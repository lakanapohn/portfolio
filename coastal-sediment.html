Landscape Architecture

Coastal Sediment Improvement for Mahachai Mangrove

This conceptual design explores how landscape architecture can slow coastal erosion, encourage sediment deposition, and rebuild mangrove habitat in Samut Sakhon, Thailand. The story is inspired by research on Samut Sakhon in the Bangkok Metropolitan Region and translates coastal processes into a layered design strategy: understand sea level rise, place adaptive tools, trap sediment, and let mangrove systems grow with community stewardship.
